EXAMPLES

Param Vikram 1000

In the 5th generation, the Param Vikram 1000 stands out as a supercomputer developed by India. Known for its high computational power, the Param Vikram 1000 contributes to scientific research and complex simulations. Designed for parallel processing, it reflects advancements in computing power and capabilities, showcasing India's commitment to technological innovation on the global stage.

The Param Vikram-1000 is the 14th fastest supercomputer in India that has 108 computing nodes dispense 7296 CPU cores, 2,76,480 GPU Cores, 74TB of RAM and 1 PB of high performance Lustre parallel filesystem.

Google's Quantum Computer (2019)

Google's quantum computer, named Sycamore, made headlines in 2019 for achieving quantum supremacy. Quantum computers leverage the principles of quantum mechanics to perform calculations that would be practically impossible for classical computers. Sycamore demonstrated the ability to solve a specific problem faster than the most advanced classical supercomputers.

While quantum computing is still in its early stages, Google's achievement marked a significant breakthrough with potential implications for solving complex problems in fields such as cryptography, optimization, and materials science.

Cray T3E (1995)

The Cray T3E was a high-performance parallel supercomputer developed by Cray Research in the mid-1990s. It was designed for scientific and engineering applications that required massive computational power. The T3E employed a scalable architecture, allowing multiple processors to work together seamlessly.

It played a significant role in advancing research in fields such as climate modeling, materials science, and fluid dynamics. The Cray T3E exemplified the capabilities of parallel processing for solving complex scientific problems.
